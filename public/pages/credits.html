CAST

Asia Bowman(she/her) is an actor from Edmonton, Alberta currently pursuing her bachelor of arts from the University of Alberta, where she majors in drama with a minor in women and gender studies. Some past credits include E-Day (Serial Collective), Anna and the Substitute Teacher (Winnipeg and Edmonton Fringe Festival), Redwood (University of Alberta), Fawn (University of Alberta), and Pomer and Penelope (University of Alberta). When she’s not acting you can find Asia at spin class, cuddling her cats, baking bread, or spending time with family and friends. She hopes you enjoy the show!

Fatmi El Fassi El Fihri (he/him) is studying acting at the U of A. He has performed in theatre since high school and is excited to share more of himself in this production. At this time, he can be found slowly losing his sanity in the comfort of his own home.

Anthony Hunchak (he/him) leads a double life as both a performer and theatre technician. Currently, he is completing a BFA in Technical Theatre Production at the University of Alberta. His range of performance experiences include found space, mascot, and puppetry, with recent escapades including DARK (Fort Edmonton Park), and But Hark, a Voice! (Thou Art Here Theatre). On the flip side, he has worked as sound designer on Bellville and Dead Man’s Cellphone (University of Alberta), and as a Fringe venue technician for Grindstone Theatre. When he’s not working on a show he enjoys playing guitar, and finding new places to eat.

Moses Kouyaté (they/them) is an Edmonton based performer currently taking a BFA in acting at the University of Alberta after training with the Citadel Theatre. Previous acts include the Lion in The Wiz, Pan in Bat Boy: The Musical (Citadel Theatre) as well as dancing with the Lobbyists (Chinook Festival) and even touring children's theatre shows with Kompany Family Theatre. They are very grateful for their family, friends and fellow artists for remaining beacons of inspiration in these hard times, and would like to thank the cast and crew of Tracks for making this beautiful experience possible today.

Marguerite Lawler (they/them) is an actor, musician, and improviser grateful to be living and creating on Treaty 6 territory. They are a senior improviser with Rapid Fire Theatre, performing in shows like OffBook: The Improvised Musical, Murder at Makeout Point, Hatboiz, the sketch comedy troupe Chuckle Ruckus, and has made stuff up around Canada, and most recently in NYC. Marguerite’s most recent credits include Titus Bouffonius (Theatre Network), The Blank Who Stole Christmas (RFT), and Working it Out (Alberta Work Plays). Marguerite is so happy to be surrounded by this amazing team, to be back working on this show, and to zoom away on these tracks with everyone.

Hayley Moorhouse (she/her) is an Edmonton-based actor and playwright. Recent performance credits include Once We Were Queens (Neon Pomegranate), Slight of Mind (Theatre Yes), Bug (University of Alberta), The Bully Project (Concrete Theatre), Off’d on Whyte (Fox Den Collective), A Bright Room Called Day, The Lower Depths, The School for Scandal and Richard III (Studio Theatre). She debuted her solo show Bird Signs in a workshop production at Nextfest 2019. Her play Suspension had its premiere production at the 2019 Edmonton Fringe Festival, and is currently available online in podcast format as part of the Alberta Queer Calendar Project (check out queercalendar.ca to listen!). In her spare time, Hayley is an Aries and pug owner. Enjoy the show!

NIUBOI (they/them) came from space to save the world from a life of blasé mundanity. They live and work in Amiskwaciy (Edmonton) on Treaty 6 territory. They are a trans non-binary alien and use they/them pronouns. Their work combines movement, visual art, and theatrical practice to create performance, video, installation, and cabaret works that explore queerness, futurism, and joy. Their self-created works Tip Off! (dance), Glass Washrooms (theatre), The Milk Bar (film), Antiquation (installation), and NIUBOI x Earth (politi-pop concert) have been presented by Edmonton Fringe Theatre, Fluid Fest, Found Festival, The Chinook Series, Mile Zero Dance, Odd Wednesday, and Nextfest. www.niuboi.com

Mustafa Rafiq (he/him) an eclectic curator of local and international talent through Sweaty Palms & a sound composer focusing on contemporary black sound art as Family Injera, Mustafa Rafiq creates opportunities to connect seemingly different genres and audiences.

In their personal projects, Rafiq explores feelings surrounding gender and being part of a post-colonial diaspora, which fuels their focus to make sonic spaces feel both safe and authentic. Nothing is off the table.

Their compostions, installations & performances have been featured in Nuit Blanche, McLuhan Arts House, Good Women Dance Society & festivals throughout Western Canada.

TEAM

Beth Dart (she/her - Director) is an Edmonton based theatre creator and collaborator who rarely makes art in a theatre… and until now never on the internet. She is the co-artistic director of Catch the Keys Productions, best known for Dead Centre of Town, the annual site specific historically based immersive theatre experience. She is also the co-artistic producer of Common Ground Arts Society and Festival Director for Found Festival. Thank you to the ensemble for wholeheartedly jumping into the unknown, Fringe Theatre for the unending support, and you, the audience, for coming along in this beautiful experiment.

Mac Brock (he/him - Producer/Creator) is a Regina-born, Edmonton-raised writer, administrator, and improviser. Playwriting credits include Boy Trouble (NextFest/Edmonton Fringe 2019), Gone at Last (Saskatchewan Playwrights' Centre), Dry (Regina Fringe Festival), and Roughhouse (Hardly Art Theatre). He’s also been thrilled to work as an administrator with Citadel Theatre, the Canadian Improv Games, Workshop West Playwrights' Theatre, and Regina's Globe Theatre School, and Rapid Fire Theatre as an ensemble member. He hopes you enjoy the work of some incredible up-and-coming artists here in Alberta.

Elise CM Jason (they/them - Production Designer) is a queer, mixed race, multi-disciplinary artist primarily working a scenographer. They are currently living and creating in amiskawaciy (colonially known as Edmonton).

Upcoming credits include Wolves (The Maggie Tree), and Tell Us What Happened (Theatre YES). Select credits including Heisenberg (Shadow Theatre), Second Hand Dances for the Crude, Crude City (Mile Zero Dance), and CHEERS & TEARS: A Journey into Whiteness (Good Women Dance Collective), In Camera (Found Festival).

Elise holds a Bachelor of Fine Art from the University of Alberta in Drama (Theatre Design), and is a member of ADC.

Even Gilchrist (he/him - Design Assistant) is a theatre creator and designer who recently graduated from the University of Ottawa theatre department and is now pursuing his MFA in Theatre Design at the University of Alberta. His previous theatrical collaborations investigated how to use the environment and design as the catalyst for innovative, original storytelling, which led to collaborating on new works that existed in blanket forts, pub attics, and basements, along a river, inside his own home - and now for the virtual realm! In his MFA studies, his primary practice is focused on developing full production designs for theatre with set, costume, lighting, and projection.

Recent Edmonton credits: Mr. Burns, a post-electric play by Blarney Productions and You Are Here Theatre (Assistant Set Designer), Boy Trouble by Vena Amoris Projects (Production Designer - Edmonton International Fringe Festival).

Bradley King (he/him - Technical Designer) is an artist/scientist/developer that is excited to join Vena Amoris Projects for Tracks. A graduate of the University of Alberta with a BSc Specialization in Physics, Bradley aspires to integrate innovative technology into theatre. He is also the co-founder of Edmonton’s only community-based improv company, The Lemon Collective. By day, you can find him working with the team at Fringe Theatre as an information systems analyst. Bradley built the platform you viewed this show on. Neat.

Frances Girard (they/them - Production Manager)

As a creator and full-time dabbler, Frances has found themself involved in everything from festival management to performing in local drag shows. Frances is currently going into their final year in MacEwan's Theatre Production program after transferring from Arts & Cultural Management. This is their first production with Vena Amoris productions and they are beyond thrilled to be a part of this amazing team!

Izzy Bergquist (she/her - Stage Manager) holds a BFA in technical theatre, majoring in Stage Management from the University of Alberta. Revolt Again"(Studio Theatre), Stage Manager for Pinocchio and Sleeping Beauty (AMTC) and Apprentice Stage Manager for As You Like It, The Party and A Christmas Carol (Citadel Theatre).
